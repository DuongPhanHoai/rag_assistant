# Student Success Policies

## Academic Risk Policy
A student should be flagged for academic risk when the weighted average score is below 70, attendance is below 75 percent, or the student has two or more failed or borderline course indicators. Advisors should combine structured performance data with advising notes before recommending intervention.

## Attendance Policy
Students are expected to attend at least 80 percent of scheduled sessions. Attendance below 75 percent requires advisor follow-up. Attendance below 65 percent should trigger an urgent intervention plan.

## Scholarship Support Policy
Scholarship support is prioritized for students with a weighted average score of at least 85, attendance of at least 85 percent, and no overdue fee balance. Students with partial balances may still be considered if they have a documented financial need and strong academic standing.

## Financial Hold Policy
Students with overdue balances above 500 should meet financial aid before registration opens. Financial hold status should not block academic support, but it should be included in risk summaries.

## Intervention Playbook
Recommended interventions include advisor meetings, weekly lab attendance, peer tutoring, financial aid review, and study-group placement. High-risk students should receive a coordinated plan that includes both academic and non-academic blockers.
